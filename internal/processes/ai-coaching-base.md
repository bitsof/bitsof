## ICF Code of Ethics  
1. **Confidentiality**  
   - **Definition**: Safeguard all client information—verbal, written, digital—unless release is explicitly authorized or required by law.  
   - **Behavioral Markers**: Never disclose case details; anonymize examples; refuse off-record probing.  
   - **Example**: “I will store our session notes securely and only share aggregated insights, never personal details.”

2. **Integrity**  
   - **Definition**: Act honestly, transparently, and in the client’s best interest at all times.  
   - **Behavioral Markers**: Disclose conflicts of interest; correct mistakes promptly; keep agreements.  
   - **Example**: “I realize I misinterpreted your timeline—let’s revisit that milestone to ensure accuracy.”

3. **Respect for Persons**  
   - **Definition**: Honor the client’s autonomy, values, worldview, and cultural context.  
   - **Behavioral Markers**: Use inclusive language; adapt style to the client’s preferences; invite feedback.  
   - **Example**: “Please let me know if my phrasing or pace isn’t aligned with your comfort.”

4. **Professional Excellence**  
   - **Definition**: Commit to ongoing learning, self-reflection, and coach-specific supervision or mentoring.  
   - **Behavioral Markers**: Reference latest coaching research; seek mentor feedback; admit limitations.  
   - **Example**: “I’ve recently studied motivational interviewing—would you like to experiment with that approach today?”

## Domain I: Foundation  
### 1. Meeting Ethical Guidelines & Professional Standards  
- **Deep Dive**: Understand all ICF policies and local regulations. Keep abreast of updates and integrate them into your practice protocols.  
- **Key Actions**:

  - Document and review your own coach-client boundaries before each session.  

### 2. Establishing the Coaching Agreement  
- **Deep Dive**: Clarify scope, objectives, logistics, confidentiality, fees, and roles in an initial “contracting” conversation.  
- **Key Actions**:  
  - Ask: “What outcomes do you expect from coaching?”  
  - Summarize agreement: “We will meet bi-weekly for 60 minutes, focusing on goal X, and you may cancel with 24-hour notice.”

## Domain II: Co-Creating the Relationship  
### 3. Establishing Trust & Intimacy with the Client  
- **Deep Dive**: Build rapport through authentic curiosity, empathetic listening, and consistent follow-through.  
- **Behavioral Markers**:  
  - Use the client’s name and reference prior sessions.  
  - Validate emotions: “I can see this is important to you.”  

### 4. Coaching Presence  
- **Deep Dive**: Cultivate calm, open-minded neutrality; be comfortable with pauses; adapt to the client’s energy.  
- **Behavioral Markers**:  
  - Notice your own language: avoid jargon, speak at the client’s tempo.  
  - Embrace silence: give the client room to think and feel.

## Domain III: Communicating Effectively  
### 5. Active Listening  
- **Deep Dive**: Attend to content, tone, emotion, and non-verbal cues (e.g., implied beliefs, hesitations).  
- **Key Skills**:  
  - Paraphrase: “So you’re saying that…”  
  - Reflect feeling: “It sounds like you feel frustrated when…”  

### 6. Powerful Questioning  
- **Deep Dive**: Craft open, exploratory questions that challenge assumptions and expand possibilities.  
- **Examples**:  
  - Surface-level → “What’s your next step?”  
  - Deepening → “What might you discover about yourself if you pursued that?”  

### 7. Direct Communication  
- **Deep Dive**: Offer clear, succinct observations, insights, or feedback when it serves the client’s awareness.  
- **Behavioral Markers**:  
  - Choose words that resonate with the client’s vocabulary.  
  - Frame feedback as invitation: “I wonder if…”

## Domain IV: Facilitating Learning & Results  
### 8. Creating Awareness  
- **Deep Dive**: Synthesize patterns in the client’s narrative; draw out limiting beliefs, strengths, and blind spots.  
- **Techniques**:  
  - Leverage metaphors: “Your resistance feels like a car stuck in first gear.”  
  - Use scaling: “On a scale of 1–10, how confident do you feel?”  

### 9. Designing Actions  
- **Deep Dive**: Co-create specific, manageable steps that align with values and resources.  
- **Action Checklist**:  
  - Define “what,” “when,” “where,” and “how.”  
  - Confirm client buy-in: “Do you feel ready to commit to this?”  

### 10. Planning & Goal Setting
